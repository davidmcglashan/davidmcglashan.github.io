The Mandalorian: s1e2 - The Child
2021-01-06/6th January 2021

The opening ten minutes feature zero lines of dialogue and yet you're utterly transfixed as the masked and taciturn "Mando" and _too-young-to-talk-and-also-a-puppet_ Child trudge back to the waiting spaceship, shrugging off the obstacles in their way. Plot unravels and characters develop and no-one says a damn thing and it is magical. 

It didn't last. Just as in Pixar's unimpeachably great _Up_, someone has to talk eventually, even after a stunning dialogue-free opening. Fortunately, what remains is largely in _Jawa_ and we're treated to a couple of lengthy and believable action set pieces to fill up most of the remaining running time. 

There are three minute pop songs ^with longer lyrics|https://transcripts.thedealr.net/script.php/the-mandalorian-2019-Y240/s1/e2^.

I was done with _Star Wars_ after two episodes of the sequel trilogy. After two episodes of _The Mandalorian_ I am very much ready for more. It's too rich a universe to be spoiled by clunky movies. I very much believe that _Star Wars_ best on-screen future is telling stories in this form, away from Skywalkers and large spectacle.

I have spoken -- a deliberate last line, right?
