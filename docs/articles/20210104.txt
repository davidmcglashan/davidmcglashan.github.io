It wasn't bad, but it wasn't great either. And this statement continues to serve as a nice summary of Jodie Whittaker's tenure.

Of course, it's a dalek story and the daleks remain problematic to modern _Doctor Who_. We haven't had a "dalek threat invades somewhere" story in a long time. In most recent dalek stories they are secondary to the main plot or appear in cameo. They're all-powerful yet easily defeated and this diminishes their impact as a main threat. Here the real daleks are used as pest control to eradicate the faux daleks introduced by the UK's Prime Minister character - a completely redundant character - as a domestic security solution in a thinly disguised rehash of 2010's _Victory of the Daleks_.

There were also rehashes of 2019's _Resolution_ (a lone, caseless dalek attaches to a human and hilarity ensues)
