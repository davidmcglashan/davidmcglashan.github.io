DRAFT Doctor Who: Revolution of the Daleks
2021-01-04/4th January 2021

It wasn't bad, but it wasn't great either. And this statement continues to serve as a frustrating summary of Jodie Whittaker's tenure.

Of course, it's a dalek story and the daleks remain problematic to modern _Doctor Who_. We haven't had a "dalek threat invades somewhere" story in a long time because the daleks are all-powerful yet easily defeated. They no longer provide any menace. Most recent dalek stories portray them in cameo or in a secondary role: a useful tool to further some other aspect of the plot, as it here where the real daleks are used as pest control to eradicate a bunch of faux daleks. 

But this is the least of this episode's worries. In its making, somewhere along the way, the phrase "How does that make you feel?" sneaked into the script and nobody noticed until the epispde was broadcast, wherein the audience noticed and face-palmed so bad there were a lot of swollen noses in the UK on the morning of the 2nd. And where the previous Doctor spent billions of years breaking through the diamond wall of a prison, one punch at a time, Thirteen seemed content to moodily resign her agency away for two decades until an unexpected jailbreak came for her.

_Doctor Who_'s format lends itself to constant reinvention. Every week a new cast, a new location, a different story to tell. Currently, the show promises more than it can deliver and its problems seem routed in its production. We leave Graham and Ryan where we first saw them on the hills above Sheffield in a scene that craftily bookends their time on the show. But as an audience we're not really sure what we ever learned about them in nearly three years. Yaz remains with The Doctor, but we haven't learned much about her either. All of their characters have remained paper-thin across the twenty or so stories that were told around them. 

It was nice to see the Clifton Suspension Bridge though.
